Omar al-Mukhtar

His Life In Brief

  • Omar al-Mukhṭār Muḥammad bin Farḥāṭ al-Manifī was his full name.
  • Born in 1858 to a poor family in the town of Zanzur near Tobruk, Libya.
  • Considered the national hero of Libya and a symbol of resistance in the Arab and Islamic worlds.
  • Received his early education at the local mosque, before continuing his studying for 8 years at the Senussi university.
  • A teacher of the Qur'an by profession, Mukhtar was also skilled in the strategies and tactics of desert warfare.
  • Led the Libyan resistance movement against the colonial Italians for nearly twenty years (1911-1931).
  • Appointed as sheikh of Zawiyat Ayn Kalk, Chad in 1895 where he Fought against the Colonial French (1899-1902).
  • Knew local geography well and used that knowledge to advantage in battles against the Italians.
  • Led his small, highly alert groups in successful attacks, after which they would fade back into the desert terrain.
  • His struggle came to an end On 11 September 1931, when he was wounded in a battle and captured by the Italian army.
  • On 16 September 1931, Mukhtar was hanged before his followers in the Suluq POW camp at the age of 73 years.
